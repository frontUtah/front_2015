Workshops

Ben Cline
How to use motion to improve UI design

Ben Cline, Co-founder and designer at Rally Interactive (and Dribbble superhero) will be teaching us how to use motion to improve our UI design.

Cori Jones
UX 101: Usability Testing. Learn how to plan, facilitate, and analyze your own usability tests

Observing users interact with your product is hands down the best way to learn what is and what is not working and why. This session will teach you how to plan, facilitate, and analyze your own usability tests. We will cover the steps of planning, recruiting, writing tasks, facilitating, analyzing, and reporting the findings. We will discuss the steps then split into groups and put the learning into practice with a quick exercise.

Joe Reni
Modern toolsets for prototyping dynamic and interactive prototypes

This workshop will use the most modern tools for rapid application prototyping. We will discuss the 4 major prototyping design methods, including modular, conditional logic, timeline, and code. We will be introduced and discuss the pros and cons to some of the most cutting edge technologies (e.g., Origami, Framer, Axure, and more.) Participants should bring laptops and be ready to get hands on as we recreate examples using a selection of the tools.

Steven Cohn
How to Validate Demand with a Prototype

Steven will show you how to apply Lean techniques to validate demand for a new feature or new product with basic prototypes. You will learn how to avoid false positives from people who are really not customers. You will also learn how to learn who your customers really are. Steven will show you these techniques and also help you apply them to your specific product.

Talks

Cameron Moll
Cohesive UX

With the ever-increasing ubiquity of screens and devices, cohesiveness is progressively imperative to successful user experiences. Recent examples include the version notes in an update to the Apple Store app for iOS: "Now start shopping on one device and finish on another, or even on your Mac or PC." Or the observation made by a tech news site following the redesign of Spotify: "Another key factor behind the design overhaul is for Spotify to feel familiar across all platforms." There are countless other examples. This session examines what's required to deliver a cohesive, consistent user experience regardless of where the digital experience begins, continues, and ends.

Marty Cagan
The root of all evil

In this talk I’ll talk about the root of all evil, or at least, the root of most failed product efforts. I’ll highlight what I see as the root causes of countless failed products, and weak product and design results. The purpose is to shine a light on some very key differences between how the vast majority of teams work, and how the best teams work.

Wade Shearer
Everyone should work at a startup

Most product design methodologies exist to compensate for one thing: a lack of real understanding of customer needs. The unfortunate thing is that disconnect is only an issue in organizations that haven't scaled properly. At a startup, you understand your customer or you go out of business. You don't arrange to "get out of the building" because you're already doing that. Research? personas? feedback from sales, marketing, and support? You don't want sympathy—you want experience. Anything less and you're guessing—educated guesses perhaps, but you're still a step removed. In this talk, I will share real-world examples from my personal experience leading design teams at large enterprise and software companies and currently as a design executive at my second startup to illustrate how product designers and managers will never succeed by simply observing.

Paul Mayne
Panel discussion with the DayOne App team

Come listen to the DayOne team talk about building one of the most successful apps and growing a business from a single designer to a thriving business of eleven. We'll put them in the hot seat and make them answer difficult questions about starting a business, responding to customer feedback, saying no to features, innovating, and how to stay ahead of competitors.

Dave Smith
Designing for Incremental Release

We don’t build modern applications monolithically, so why do we design them this way? These days, agile product teams tend to release features early and often, even if the features belong to a broader product that is not finished. If done right, the design process can support this. This talk will show you how we’ve done this at HireVue over the past few years.

Clayton Farr
Off The Garden Path - Confirming What Matters Most

When we pretend to use a pretend thing, we think we know what we like, and what we would do, but we’re very often wrong. It’s our actions in real situations that reveal what we truly value. To base our design decisions on what people actually need and want we have to make prototypes that can be used off the garden path – that behave like fully working applications. And we need to make them as readily as we create our current high-fidelity versions. Thankfully, now we can. In this talk, Clayton will introduce tools, example projects, and an approach that places the confirmation of value at the front, and center, of your design process.

Lauren Gray
Battle of the Sexes

How different is the customer journey for men and women? Devices, browsing habits, purchases... we’ll explore the trends and draw the best conclusions possible.

Peter Merholz
Shaping organizations to deliver great user experiences

Design and UX have won. Companies are building in-house teams like never before. Unfortunately, they're doing so with outdated organizational models, and so are not getting the most out of their design and UX investment (in fact, some companies are getting negative value because of how UX is placed within the organization). In this presentation, I will use my personal story of growing a design organization from 30 to 60 as a way to explore how to shape organizations to deliver great user experiences. We'll delve into matters of organizational models, team structures, and processes to identify how your organization can be most effective.

Matt Snyder
How to eat an elephant - finding design success on large-scale design projects.

The aspects of large-scale design projects can strike fear into the hearts of the strongest designers – multiple stakeholders, unknown constraints, and long road-maps… it's enough to make anyone break into a cold sweat and go to their happy place. Learn tips and techniques for keeping your head above water, breaking down large-scale problems, and increase success as you provide important design value on large projects that probably need UX the most.

Jay Fichialos
It’s Always Happy Hour Somewhere - The Future of Product Design Organizations

As top talent becomes harder to acquire and internet access more widespread, organizations are turning to a new model for building Product Design teams. This will be a hands on discussion about the structure and principles that help foster great teams and are defining the future of Product Design. Sharing stories, tools and techniques from the front lines of a global design team, we’ll outline keys to successfully participating or leading a distributed organization. How to recruit top talent? How to collaborate, support and maintain accountability? How to develop careers? And what to do when your team member joins a meeting from the bedroom, street café, or bicycle race.

Archana Thiagarajan
Leadership by Design: Begin an enterprise experience revolution

Why is designing for enterprise application so different than that of consumer experience design? What are the myths? What are the things you can do to change the business, the culture and approach? How do you penetrate enterprise environments to make experience be the differentiator and not the compromise? Join me to to talk about real life examples of how we are creating a small movement, driven by designers and watching it turn into a leadership movement that will help transform the marketing cloud business for Adobe.

Gilbert Lee
Designing the Product vs. Designing the Experience

Businesses are being asked to do more than just create products. They are needing to build experiences that may require them to change their businesses. Learn how to adopt to a new set of customer needs in this age of startups, mobile, smarter businesses, and fast-moving technology. Gilbert Lee will share his experiences in developing products from research, design, development, and launch.

Nate Walkingshaw
How Do I Work That Way! Inspiring organizations to change

In this presentation, Nate will talk about his process for developing and launching great products.

Inga Johnson
4 common habits that kill the power of data

Everyone nods their head when you ask if they want their department to be built on a strong foundation of data and customer insights. Yet the painful reality is that most of us work in an environment that is not achieving this goal. From my experience in both start-ups and billion dollar companies, there are a shared set of mistakes that keep companies from really embracing the power of data. Here are four trends that I have seen and my own wins (and losses) in trying to overcome these obstacles.
